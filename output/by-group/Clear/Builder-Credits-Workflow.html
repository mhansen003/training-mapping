üèóÔ∏è What Are Builder Credits?

Builder credits are incentives offered by home builders to attract buyers. These credits can be applied toward closing costs, rate buydowns, or upgrades. Proper documentation is critical for compliance and accurate Closing Disclosure preparation.

Credit Type Common Uses Max Allowed
Closing Cost Credit Buyer closing costs, prepaids Based on LTV/loan type
Rate Buydown Temporary or permanent rate reduction Per investor guidelines
Upgrade Credit Builder-installed upgrades Per contract
Price Reduction Reduction from list price Must be documented

Credit Types & Limits

Understanding IPC limits by loan type

üìä Interested Party Contribution (IPC) Limits

Builder credits are considered Interested Party Contributions. Maximum limits vary by loan type and LTV:

Loan Type LTV ‚â§ 75% LTV 75.01-90% LTV > 90%
Conventional 9% of sales price 6% of sales price 3% of sales price
FHA 6% of sales price (all LTVs)
VA 4% of sales price (closing costs only)
USDA 6% of sales price

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Excess Credits

Credits exceeding IPC limits cannot be applied to closing costs and may require price adjustment. Always verify credit amounts against applicable limits before entering.
