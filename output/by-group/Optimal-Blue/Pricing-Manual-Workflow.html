Accessing Pricing - Clear & Byte

Optimal Blue is CMG's pricing engine, accessible through both Clear and Byte LOS systems. Understanding the different access methods and their implications is critical for accurate pricing and loan management.

Accessing Pricing from Clear

1
Click the Pricing Shortcut
Navigate to the loan and click the Pricing shortcut in the Clear interface. This opens Optimal Blue with two tabs: Pricing (default) and What if Pricing.
2
Choose Your Mode
Pricing Tab: Default mode with all functions. Click Search for full pricing capabilities.
What if Pricing: Access Loan Scenario and Add Scenario functions. Changes in scenarios do NOT flow back to the loan.
3
Click Search
For full pricing functions, click the Search button. Optimal Blue opens and displays Eligible and Ineligible Products and Programs.

Accessing Pricing from Byte

1
Open Pricing and Lock Request
In Byte, navigate to the Pricing and Lock Request form. Two tabs are available: What if as Loan Officer and Submit as Loan Officer.
2
Choose Submission Type
What if as Loan Officer: Access all scenario functions without risk of data flowing back to the loan.
Submit as Loan Officer: For full functions and all loan options.
3
Submit for Pricing
Leave on Pricing, then click Submit to view full pricing results. Loan information auto-populates in the Optimal Blue search window.
⚠
Critical: Data Flow Warning
Any changes needed to loan parameters on unlocked loans should be made in the Clear/Byte file itself, NOT on the OB Search page. Data changed in the loan fields in OB DO push back to the loan.

Pricing Multiple Scenarios Simultaneously

Optimal Blue allows you to compare multiple loan scenarios side-by-side, helping borrowers understand their options and make informed decisions.

1
Add Scenario
Click + Add Scenario prior to pressing the Submit button on the OB input screen. You can create multiple scenarios for comparison.
2
Name Each Scenario
Give each scenario a descriptive name (e.g., "Purchase 5% Down", "Purchase 10% Down", "Conventional 5% Down") to better identify it in search results.
3
Configure Loan Information
For each scenario, configure the loan parameters:
  • Lien Position (First Lien, Second Lien)
  • Loan Type (Conventional, FHA, VA, etc.)
  • Base Loan Amount
  • Appraised Value
  • Subordinate Financing (if applicable)
4
Submit Search
Click Search to view pricing results for all scenarios. Results are displayed with scenario names, making comparison easy.
💡
Best Practice
Use descriptive scenario names that include the key differentiator (down payment %, loan type, etc.) to quickly identify options in the results screen. The "Include Scenarios" dropdown allows filtering results by specific scenarios.

The search results will display each scenario's eligible products with columns for Rate, Price, Discount/Rebate, Lock Days, and P&I payment.

Search Results Screen

The Search Results screen displays all eligible and ineligible loan programs based on the loan criteria entered.

Understanding the Results

  • Eligible Products: Displayed below the blue banner, showing all programs the loan qualifies for
  • Ineligible Products: Appear below the list of eligible products
  • Disqualifiers Column: Review this column to understand why a product is marked ineligible

Eligible Products Screen Columns

Column Description
Product Name Click to display rate options, lock periods, price adjustments, and advisories
Rate Interest rate for the product
Price Price as a percentage of the loan amount
Discount/Rebate Points charged or credit received (negative = credit)
Lock Days Number of days the rate is locked
P&I Principal and Interest payment amount
1
Review Ineligible Products
If a product is marked as ineligible, review the Disqualifiers column to understand why.
2
Correct Loan Criteria
Click the Product Search icon (pencil) to review and correct loan criteria, OR return to the Clear URLA screen to make corrections in the loan file.
3
Re-run Search
After making changes, re-run the Search for qualifying products.
📝
Important Note
Search results changes in fields in OB do NOT automatically update the file when the loan is locked, except when performed as a Profile Change on a locked loan.

Rate Stack & Product Details

After selecting a product, you can view the complete rate stack with all available interest rates and detailed pricing information.

1
Select a Product
Click on a product name to open the rate stack and view all available interest rates. The rate stack shows Rate, Price, P&I, MI (Mortgage Insurance), and Discount/Rebate for each rate option.
2
Select Lock Period
Choose your lock period from the available options (All, 21, 30, 45, 60 days). Different lock periods may have different pricing.
3
Review Rate Details
Click the "i" icon for the desired interest rate. Product and Pricing details are displayed, along with an example payment schedule including estimated Mortgage Insurance quote if applicable.

Rate Details Panel

Field Description
Rate Interest rate (e.g., 7.125%)
Price Price as percentage (e.g., 100.137)
Discount/Rebate Points charged or credit (e.g., -0.137% / ($616))
P&I Monthly Principal & Interest payment
Lock Period Number of days rate is guaranteed
Base Loan Amount Original loan amount
Total Loan Amount Final loan amount including financed fees
Prepaid Finance Charge Prepaid interest and finance charges

PMI Quote Tool

Click the MI icon for the target interest rate to get a general PMI quote based on current OB field information from designated PMI vendors.

⚠
PMI Quote Requirement
To achieve a full and correct quote exact to the loan, the user must run GET PMI in Clear within the loan, or PMI Get quote in Byte within the loan, for a bona fide quote. Obtaining the loan is also the only way to populate the PMI factor field and enable auto-quote flow to Clear docs, as required and by design.

Originator Assistant

Originator Assistant is an intelligent tool that analyzes the best-priced product and provides suggestions for borrowers to obtain better loan terms.

How It Works

Originator Assistant examines the following parameters within specific ranges and shows the effect on Rate, Price, and Discount/Rebate:

Parameter Analysis Range Impact Shown
LTV (Loan-to-Value) +/- 5% Rate, Price, Discount/Rebate
Loan Amount Various thresholds Rate, Price, Discount/Rebate
FICO Score +/- 10 points Rate, Price, Discount/Rebate
DTI (Debt-to-Income) +/- 5% Rate, Price, Discount/Rebate
1
Access Originator Assistant
Click the Originator Assistant tab on the search results screen. It automatically analyzes the best-priced product (first product returned).
2
Select Alternative Products
Use the dropdown menu to select alternative products to analyze opportunities to improve pricing.
3
Review Scenarios
Review the comparison table showing the Current scenario versus Proposed Changes with their impact on Rate, Price, Discount/Rebate, Lock Days, and P&I.
💡
Pro Tip
Use Originator Assistant to identify if small changes (like a $50 increase in loan amount or 10-point credit score improvement) could significantly improve the borrower's pricing.

Save the Loan (Float) Without Locking

If you want to save the pricing information to the LOS without locking the interest rate, use the Update LOS function.

1
Navigate to Lock Form
After selecting your desired rate and product, proceed to the Lock Form.
2
Click Update LOS
Click Update LOS to update the LOS without locking or floating the interest rate. This saves the product and pricing information to the loan file.
💡
When to Float
Use the float option when:
  • The borrower wants to wait for potentially better rates
  • You need to save current pricing for comparison later
  • The loan is not yet ready for lock (pending conditions)

Request Lock with Concession

Price concessions allow for price improvements or competitive matching. Understanding the difference between Net LC and General LC is critical.

Types of Lender Credits

Type Description Use Case
Net LC Price-improvement adjustment typically used to reduce discount points charged to borrower. Can also be used toward closing costs if resulting in price above par. Reducing borrower costs without disclosure as a specific credit
General LC Lump-sum credit itemized on the LE and CD. Once disclosed, cannot be reduced or removed. When a specific credit amount must be disclosed to borrower
1
Access Concession Request
Click the dropdown next to Request Lock, then click Request Lock with Concession.
2
Select Price Concession Reason
Select the appropriate reason from the dropdown (e.g., Price Match with Competition, Lock Extension, etc.).
3
Enter Concession Amount
Enter either:
  • Price Concession Amount: Dollar or Percentage amount of desired concession
  • Desired Price with Concession: Enter the desired Final price to calculate the required concession amount
4
Upload Supporting Documents
Click Add files to upload to attach Supporting Documents. For Competitive Price Match options, the Competitive Price Match Request Form (fillable PDF) is required.
5
Add Comments
Enter comments to the approver regarding the justification for the price concession and/or comments for the lock desk (net vs general LC, etc.).
6
Submit Request
Click Submit to send the concession request for approval.
📄 Competitive Price Match Request Form (PDF)
⚠
General LC Warning
Once a General Lender Credit is disclosed to the borrower on the LE and CD, it cannot be reduced or removed. Use carefully and only when appropriate.

Post-Lock Changes - Profile Change

Profile Changes are used to modify locked loans, particularly fields that may affect pricing or product eligibility. This includes loan program changes such as moving from FHA to Conventional or vice versa.

Available Change Request Options

  • Lock Extension - Extend the lock period
  • Profile Change - Modify locked loan fields
  • Price Concession - Request pricing adjustment
  • Float Down - Take advantage of improved market rates
  • Relock - Re-lock an expired rate lock
1
Click Profile Change
In Clear, click Profile Change on the Pricing Screen. The Change Request menu will appear.
2
Make Desired Changes
Make the desired changes on the OB Search screen:
  • Loan Amount
  • Property Type
  • Appraised Value
  • Product/Program
3
Submit for Updated Pricing
When satisfied that the terms are correct, click Submit. Optimal Blue displays updated pricing considering any updates made.
4
Select New Interest Rate
Select the desired interest rate from the note stack and click Select.
5
Review Change Summary
Optimal Blue provides a summary of the changes, side by side with the original loan structure, to compare and verify accuracy. Review the Change Request Details showing original values vs. changed values.
6
Re-commit the Loan
Click Submit at the top of the page to re-commit the loan with the changes.
📝
Change Summary Details
The summary shows: LTV, CLTV, HCLTV, Base Loan Amount, Total Loan Amount, APOR, Price, Start/Par Price, Start/Par Rate, P&I, and any Adjustment Reasons with their impact on Points, Rate, and Margin.

Price Concession (Post-Lock)

Price concessions on locked loans follow a specific approval workflow. An email is automatically sent to the appropriate approver.

1
Select Price Concession
From the Change Request menu, select Price Concession.
2
Select First Lien
Select First Lien as the lien position.
3
Complete Required Fields
  • Price Concession Reason: Select from dropdown (Net LC or General LC)
  • Price Concession Amount: Dollar or Percentage amount
  • OR Desired Price with Concession: Enter desired final price
4
Upload Supporting Documents
Click Upload Files to attach supporting documents. The Competitive Price Match Request Form (fillable PDF) is required for price match requests.
5
Enter Justification Comments
Enter comments to the approver regarding the justification for the price concession.
6
Submit Request
Click Submit Request. An email is automatically generated and sent to the approver with appropriate authority.
💡
Approval Notification
The Loan Officer will receive an email once the price concession is approved and applied in the system. The Concession Activity panel shows Total Applied and Denied Requests history.
📄 Competitive Price Match Request Form (PDF)

Lock Extension

Lock extensions allow you to extend the rate lock period when more time is needed to close the loan.

1
Select Lock Extension
From within the pricing screen, select Lock Extension.
2
Enter Extension Days
Enter the desired number of days to extend the lock (e.g., 4 days).
3
Review Extension Details
The system displays:
  • Calculated Cost: The cost of the extension (e.g., -0.08)
  • Current Value vs. With Extension: Comparison of Price, Lock Days, Lock Expiration, and Discount/Rebate
4
Apply Extension
Click Apply to execute the lock extension, or Cancel to abort.
📝
Extension Impact Example
A 4-day extension might change: Price from 100.403 to 100.323, Lock from 30 to 34 days, Lock Expiration from 6/5/25 to 6/9/25, and Discount/Rebate from ($1,914.25) / -0.403% to ($1,534.25) / -0.323%.

Float Down

Float Down allows borrowers to take advantage of improved market rates after locking, subject to policy requirements.

1
Select Float Down
From within the Pricing Screen, select Float Down.
2
Click Single Lock Period Tab
Click the Single Lock Period tab to view available rates.
3
Expand Note Stack
Click the program name to expand the note stack and view all available rates.
4
Select New Rate
Click Select for the desired rate. OB displays a review page showing change details.
5
Review and Confirm
Review the Change Request Details comparing original values to changed values. Verify the float-down benefits the borrower.
⚠
Policy Compliance Required
Consult the Retail Lending Lock Desk Policy to confirm the float-down meets policy requirements. Optimal Blue does not evaluate whether a float down is valid or "worth it" to the customer. It includes the float-down fee in the pricing.
📄 Retail Lending Lock Desk Policy

Re-lock (Expired Lock)

Re-lock is used when a rate lock has expired and needs to be re-established. Re-locked loans use worst-case pricing and include relock fees.

Relock Fee Schedule

Lock Term (Days) Relock Fee
10 days 0.000
21 days 0.125
30 days 0.250
45 days 0.375
1
Select Relock
On a loan with an expired rate lock, select Relock.
2
Select First Lien
Select First Lien as the lien position.
3
Click Select Rate and Price
Click Select Rate and Price to view available options.
4
Select Interest Rate
Select the desired interest rate from the note stack and click the Lock icon.
5
Review Comparison Summary
Optimal Blue provides a summary comparing changes side by side with the Original Lock. OB identifies original locked pricing vs. current pricing and shows that pricing plus any applicable relock fees.
6
Request Lock
Click Request Lock to submit the relock request.
⚠
Worst-Case Pricing
Re-locked loans are based on worst-case pricing between the original lock date and current market rates. Final pricing includes all prior extension and/or relock fees. The original lock term will be used to determine pricing.
📄 Retail Lending Lock Desk Policy
