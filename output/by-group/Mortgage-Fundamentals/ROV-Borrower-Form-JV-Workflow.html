What is a Reconsideration of Value (ROV)?

A Reconsideration Request may be submitted when additional market information exists or additional facts about the subject's physical condition exist that were not considered and/or included in the original appraisal. The completion of a Reconsideration request may or may not result in a change to the report or the value opinion.

Critical Limitation

One ROV can be submitted per Appraisal. Ensure all relevant comparables and information are included in the single submission.

Need Assistance?

If you need help completing the form, reach out to your Loan Officer or Account Manager. After completion, submit the form to your Processing Point of Contact who will route your request for review.

Purpose Options

  • Option 1: Review additional sold comparables which sold prior to the effective date of the Appraisal Report
  • Option 2: Correct and/or explain any factual errors (comment required below)
  • Option 3: Appraisal may have been influenced by Bias or Discrimination (comment required below)

Multiple Purposes

You may select more than one purpose if applicable. For Options 2 and 3, you must provide detailed comments explaining the factual errors or concerns about bias/discrimination in the designated comment section.

Bias or Discrimination Concerns

If you believe the appraisal may have been influenced by bias or discrimination, provide specific details about your concerns. CMG Financial takes these matters seriously and will investigate all claims thoroughly.

Comparable Sale Criteria

  • Timing: Must be a closed sale no more than 12 months prior to the effective date of the Appraisal
  • Date Restriction: Comparable sales cannot be after the effective date of the appraisal
  • Characteristics: Should have similar characteristics including GLA, room/bedroom/bathroom count, age, condition, quality of construction, and style (but do not have to be identical)
  • Proximity: Should be proximate to your home - the closer and more similar, the more likely to be accepted

Best Comparables

If you live in a subdivision, condominium project, or PUD, recent sales in your development of similar homes are the most persuasive comparables you can provide.

Proximity Guidelines

While proximity requirements may vary based on whether your location is rural or urban, keep in mind that the closer (and more similar in characteristics) the comparable, the more likely it will be considered appropriate and acceptable.

What Happens Next?

After submission, your Processing Point of Contact will route the ROV request for review. The appraiser will evaluate the proposed comparables and supporting information. Note that completion of a Reconsideration request may or may not result in a change to the report or the value opinion.

Important Reminder

Remember: Only one ROV can be submitted per appraisal. Make sure to include all relevant comparables and information in your initial submission, as you will not have another opportunity to submit additional requests for this appraisal.

# Reconsideration of Value (ROV) - Borrower Form

## Overview
A Reconsideration Request may be submitted when additional market information exists or additional facts about the subject's physical condition exist that were not considered and/or included in the original appraisal.

## Key Limitations
- ONE ROV per appraisal
- Completion may or may not result in value change

## Acceptable Comparables
- Closed sales only
- Within 12 months prior to appraisal effective date
- Cannot be after effective date
- Similar characteristics (GLA, beds, baths, age, condition)
- Proximate to subject property
- Minimum 3, maximum 5 comparables recommended

## Purpose Options
1. Review additional sold comparables
2. Correct/explain factual errors
3. Bias or discrimination concerns

## Comparable Data Required
- Property Address
- MLS Listing Number
- Proximity to Subject
- Beds / Bath / Site Size / GLA
- Basement details
- Sale Value and Close Date
- Comments (optional)

## Submission Process
1. Complete form with all required fields
2. Submit to Processing Point of Contact
3. POC routes request for appraiser review

## Support
Contact Loan Officer or Account Manager for assistance
