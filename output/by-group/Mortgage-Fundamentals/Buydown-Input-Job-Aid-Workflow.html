Program Overview

Understanding the Settle-In Prepaid Buydown concept

What is Settle-In Prepaid Buydown?

The Settle-In Prepaid Buydown is CMG's creative use of seller concessions to prepay part of a borrower's monthly mortgage payment. It functions similarly to a temporary rate buydown but does not affect the rate or APR. Funds are placed in a subsidy account, and servicing draws the same amount each month to reduce the borrower's payment.

Flex Cash provides payment relief over time, not at closing. This is a key distinction from traditional closing cost credits.

Eligibility & Core Concept

Program requirements and how the buydown works
Eligible Programs Ineligible Programs
✓ FHA, VA, USDA (Ginnie Mae loans) ✗ Conventional
✗ Any DPAs, housing agency loans, or private investors
✗ Temporary rate buydowns (cannot be combined)

Key Program Rules

  • Permanent rate buydowns ARE allowed in conjunction with Settle-In
  • Seller concessions must still comply with maximum IPC limits per program
  • Borrower funds used to prepay principal must be seasoned and not counted toward the minimum down payment

Core Concept - How It Works

  • Subsidy Account: Seller concessions fund a subsidy account
  • Level Payment: Servicing draws the same amount each month for the duration of the buydown
  • Principal Restriction: Funds cannot be applied to principal - only interest, taxes, insurance, or mortgage insurance
  • Flexible Duration: From 1 to 36 months (unlike 12/24/36 in temporary buydowns)
  • Optional Principal Prepay: Borrowers may choose to prepay principal using their own funds to temporarily "pause" monthly payments, but this prepayment does not apply toward the minimum required investment

Loan Officer Responsibilities

  • Step 1: Discuss program options with borrower
  • Step 2: Determine if borrower wants to use seller concessions, own funds, or both
  • Step 3: Complete and provide the Prepaid Buydown Calculator Worksheet using the CMG Settle-In Mortgage Calculator
  • Step 4: Upload worksheet to the loan file
  • Step 5: Attest to completing the required training before originating

Processor / Underwriter Responsibilities

  • Verify: Loan is eligible (FHA/VA/USDA only)
  • Verify: Seller concessions do not exceed IPC limits
  • Verify: Borrower's prepaid principal funds are seasoned and separate from down payment
  • Ensure: Calculator worksheet is in the file
  • Confirm: Prepaid Buydown box is checked on the Prepaid Buydown Screen in Byte (ensures Buydown Agreement prints with doc package)

Closer / Funder Responsibilities

  • Input: Total buydown amount as a lump sum in Section H ("Paid to CMG")
  • Record: If borrower prepays principal, record it separately (borrower's own funds)
  • Net: Each buydown line item from the wire
  • Ensure: Prepaid Buydown checkbox is selected - this sends data to Servicing
  • Confirm: Buydown Agreement populates correctly and is included in the doc package

The Prepaid Buydown screen in Byte is used to set up the subsidy account details so Servicing can withdraw a level amount each month to reduce the borrower's payment.

Critical: Payment Restrictions

  • The monthly buydown amount must remain the same each month
  • Can only be applied to portions of the payment that are not principal (interest, taxes, insurance, and MI)
  • If borrower is attempting to "pause payments," borrower may contribute their own seasoned funds to cover the principal portion

Buydown Options (1-4)

Four scenario options for borrower payment reduction

Option 1: Payment Reduction (Pause Payments)

  • ✓ Purpose: Cover borrower's full monthly payment for a set period
  • ✓ How it works: Seller concessions fund non-principal portion
  • ✓ Borrower: Brings seasoned funds to cover principal
  • ✓ Ops confirm: Level payment each month, borrower funds documented separately

Option 2: Front-Load (Max Up Front)

  • ✓ Purpose: Largest monthly payment reduction upfront
  • ✓ How it works: Higher monthly subsidy over fewer months
  • ✓ Ops confirm: Monthly subsidy doesn't exceed non-principal limit
  • ✓ Note: Borrower still pays principal portion

Option 3: Fixed Months

  • ✓ Purpose: Payment reduction for specific number of months
  • ✓ How it works: Worksheet calculates monthly subsidy needed
  • ✓ Borrower chooses: The timeframe (1-36 months)
  • ✓ Ops confirm: Months within limits, principal covered

Option 4: Fixed Amount

  • ✓ Purpose: Reduce payment by specific dollar amount/month
  • ✓ How it works: Worksheet calculates how many months subsidy supports
  • ✓ Ops confirm: Total funds support selected amount
  • ✓ Limits: Months don't exceed 36, principal not subsidized

Verification Process

On the Amortization Schedule Screen, compare the Princ. Red. column amount for the Total # of buydown months. Confirm that the borrower's monthly payment during buydown is greater than the principal portion of the borrower's payment.

Example Verification

16 Total # of Buydown Months:
Borrower's monthly payment during buydown is $3,761.74.
That is greater than the principal of $481.28.
Calculator should also confirm this validation.

Compliance & Operational Tips

Timing requirements and best practices

Compliance & Timing

  • No new disclosures or 3-day TRID wait (prepaids, not fees)
  • If the compliance test fails, adjust the GFE block to "None"
  • Funds from seller concessions count toward the VA 4% max if applicable
  • At payoff/refi, any remaining subsidy funds are applied to principal

Operational Tips

  • Keep seller credits as lump sums for easier IPC tracking
  • Push back on LOs asking ops to calculate scenarios - use the calculator tool
  • Double-check principal coverage using the Amortization Schedule
  • Feedback encouraged - this workflow is new and subject to refinement

## Overview
The Settle-In Prepaid Buydown is CMG's creative use of seller concessions to prepay part of a borrower's monthly mortgage payment. It functions similarly to a temporary rate buydown but does not affect the rate or APR.

## Program Eligibility
- ELIGIBLE: FHA, VA, USDA (Ginnie Mae loans)
- INELIGIBLE: Conventional, DPAs, Housing Agency Loans, Private Investors
- Cannot combine with temporary rate buydowns
- Permanent rate buydowns ARE allowed

## Core Concept

- Servicing draws SAME amount each month
- Funds applied to: Interest, Taxes, Insurance, MI
- Funds CANNOT be applied to: Principal
- Duration: 1-36 months (flexible)
