HIGH RISK: Underwriting Implications

Sole proprietorships highlight the highest risk of all classifications due to owner's unlimited personal liability for business debts and losses.

Risks to Look For

  • Non-recurring other income or losses - May inflate or deflate true income
  • Reserves for self-employment - Ensure adequate cash reserves
  • Accrual method accounting - Distorts the borrower's cash flow and revenue

Verification Requirements

  • Verify phone number & address online
  • Verify business license/corporate commission
  • CPA letter

Additional Income Types

Other self-employed income sources and special considerations
Business Type Tax Forms Risks to Look For
Rental Income Part I of Schedule E on Form 1040 Depreciation related to income (or loss) from rentals must be added back to net gain (or loss)
Capital Gains & Losses Schedule D on Form 1040 One-time transaction - Not usually considered as either a gain or loss in determining income. If history of consistent turnover of assets, use of gain or loss may be considered.
Farm Income or Loss Schedule F on Form 1040 Profit or loss from farming. Any depreciation shown on Schedule F must be added back to the adjusted gross income.
Social Security Page 1 of Form 1040 Can be included if income will continue to be received for at least three years. Non-taxable portion must be added back to the adjusted gross income.
Employee Business Expenses - These are actual out-of-pocket expenses that should be deducted from the borrower's adjusted gross income.

Important: Depreciation Add-Backs

For Rental Income and Farm Income, depreciation must be added back to the net gain or loss when calculating qualifying income.

Social Security Continuance

Social Security income can only be included if there is documentation that the income will continue to be received for at least three years. Remember to add back the non-taxable portion to adjusted gross income.

# Self-Employed Borrower Job Aid

## Overview
Self-employed income exists in a variety of ways. Borrowers with self-employed income require additional income verification. Always review AUS Findings and Underwriting Guidelines for additional details. Search "self-employed" in ThinkItHub for full details.

## Business Types

### 1. Sole Proprietor (HIGHEST RISK)
- Forms: Schedule C of Form 1040
- Taxation: Owner's personal rate
- Risk: Unlimited personal liability for business debts

### 2. Partnership (General/Limited)
- Forms: Schedule E Part II + K-1, Form 1065
- Taxation: Pass-through to partners
- Risk: General partners have unlimited liability; Limited partners have limited liability

### 3. S Corporation (LOW RISK)
- Forms: Schedule E Part II + K-1, Form 1120S
- Taxation: Pass-through, no corporate tax
- Risk: Liability limited to investment

### 4. Corporation (LOW RISK)
- Forms: W-2 + Schedule B, Form 1120
- Taxation: Double taxation (corporate + personal)
- Risk: Liability limited to investment

### 5. 1099 Employee
- Forms: Form 1040 + Form 1099
- Risks: Meals & Entertainment expenses, earnings trends

## Risks to Analyze
- Non-recurring income/losses
- Consistency of revenue and profitability
- Positive sales and earnings trends
- Accrual method accounting issues
- Reserves for self-employment
- Liquidity to support withdrawals

## Verification Requirements (All Types)
1. Verify phone number & address online
2. Verify business license/corporate commission
3. CPA letter (or reference letter for 1099)

## Depreciation Add-Backs
- Rental Income (Schedule E): Add back to net gain/loss
- Farm Income (Schedule F): Add back to adjusted gross income
