Document Purpose

The Credit Inquiry Letter is a standardized CMG Financial form used to obtain signed, dated letters of explanation from borrowers for all credit inquiries on their credit report made within the last 120 days. This documentation is required for underwriting compliance.

Key Requirement

The borrower must explain the reason for each inquiry AND indicate whether any new debt was obtained from the credit pull. If debt was obtained, supporting documentation is required.

Important: All Inquiries Need Explanation

Even if the borrower did not obtain new debt, an explanation is still required. The borrower must check "No" for debt obtained and provide a brief explanation (e.g., "Shopping for rates," "Did not proceed with application," "For insurance quote only").

Form Completion Methods

The form can be completed in two ways:

  • Digital: Fill out by typing in the spaces provided, then print, sign, and return
  • Manual: Print the form and manually write in the explanations

Clear Form Function

The form includes a "CLICK HERE TO CLEAR FORM" button that resets all fields. Use this if you need to start over or correct multiple entries.

When "Yes" is Selected - Documentation Required

  • Account Statement: Provide a copy of the most recent account statement showing the account number, balance, and payment amount
  • Terms of Obligation: Alternatively, provide the terms/contract showing the loan amount, interest rate, monthly payment, and term length
  • Payment Verification: If payments have been made, provide evidence of payment history
Debt Type Required Documentation Impact on Loan
Auto Loan Loan agreement or first statement Added to DTI calculation
Credit Card Card agreement and current statement Added to DTI (minimum payment)
Personal Loan Loan agreement showing terms Added to DTI calculation
Retail Financing Financing agreement or statement May affect DTI if payments due
Mortgage Inquiry Closing Disclosure if closed Full analysis required

Critical: New Debt Affects Loan Qualification

Any new debt obtained must be factored into the debt-to-income (DTI) ratio calculation. This may require:

  • Re-running AUS (Automated Underwriting System)
  • Updated DTI calculations by the processor
  • Possible loan restructuring if DTI exceeds guidelines
  • Communication with underwriting about the new liability

  • Primary Borrower: Must sign and date the document in the first signature line
  • Co-Borrower: If applicable, must sign and date in the second signature line
  • Date Requirement: The date must be current (the day the borrower signs)
  • Signature Consistency: Signature should match other loan documents

Electronic Signatures

If using e-signatures, ensure the document is signed through an approved e-signature platform that meets E-Sign Act compliance requirements.

Missing Signature Rejection

A Credit Inquiry Letter without a valid signature and date will be rejected by underwriting. Ensure both elements are present before submitting to the loan file.

Common Scenarios & Explanations

Sample explanations for typical credit inquiries

Mortgage Rate Shopping (Multiple Inquiries)

When borrowers shop for the best mortgage rate and have multiple lender inquiries.

Sample Explanation

"This inquiry was for mortgage rate shopping as I was comparing rates from multiple lenders for my home purchase. I did not obtain a loan from this lender."

Auto Loan Rate Shopping

Multiple auto dealer or finance company inquiries within a short period.

Sample Explanation - No Debt

"This inquiry was from shopping for auto loan rates. I was comparing financing options but did not purchase a vehicle from this dealer."

Sample Explanation - Debt Obtained

"This inquiry resulted in an auto loan for the purchase of a 2024 Honda Accord. Monthly payment is $425. Statement attached."

Credit Card Application

Retail or bank credit card inquiries.

Sample Explanation - Declined

"Applied for a credit card but was declined. No new account was opened."

Sample Explanation - Approved

"Applied for and received a new credit card with a $5,000 limit. Current balance is $0. Statement attached."

Utility / Service Setup

Inquiries from utility companies or service providers.

Sample Explanation

"This inquiry was for setting up new electric service at my rental property. No credit was extended; this was a credit check for service deposit purposes only."

Insurance Quote

Soft or hard pulls from insurance companies.

Sample Explanation

"This inquiry was for obtaining an auto/home insurance quote. I was comparing insurance rates. No debt or credit was obtained."

Employment Background Check

Inquiries from employers for pre-employment screening.

Sample Explanation

"This inquiry was for a pre-employment background check required by my new employer. No credit was obtained or applied for."

Writing Effective Explanations

Good explanations should include:

  • The reason for the inquiry (what you were shopping for)
  • The outcome (approved, declined, did not proceed)
  • Whether any debt/credit was obtained
  • If debt was obtained, the basic terms (amount, payment)

Credit Inquiry Letter Workflow Documentation

# Credit Inquiry Letter - CMG Financial

## Overview
A signed and dated letter of explanation is needed for all credit inquiries on a borrower's credit report made within the last 120 days.

## Purpose
- Document borrower explanations for recent credit inquiries
- Identify any new debt obtained from credit pulls
- Ensure compliance with underwriting requirements

## Form Requirements

### Header Information
- Loan Number
- Borrower Name

### For Each Inquiry
- Inquiry Date/Lender name
- Debt Obtained: Yes or No (checkbox)
- Explanation of the inquiry reason

### Signatures
- Borrower Signature with Date
- Co-Borrower Signature with Date (if applicable)

## If Debt Was Obtained
When "Yes" is checked for debt obtained:
- Provide account statement OR
- Provide terms of the obligation document
- New debt must be factored into DTI calculation

## Common Inquiry Types
- Auto Dealer/Finance Company
- Bank/Credit Union
- Mortgage Lender
- Retail Store
- Utility Company
- Insurance Company
- Employer Background Check

## Submission
- Return completed form to Loan Officer or Processor
- Include supporting documentation if debt obtained
- Email preferred (PDF attachment)
- Include loan number in subject line
