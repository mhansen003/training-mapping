Year-to-Date Requirement

Year to date (YTD) income should always be supported on the paystub for all pay frequency types. This ensures accurate income verification.

Weekly Pay

  • + Hours Indicator: 37.5 or 40 hours
  • + Pay Periods: 52 per year
Weekly Gross x 52 / 12 = Monthly
Example: $600/week x 52 / 12 = $2,600/month

Bi-Weekly Pay

  • + Hours Indicator: 80 hours
  • + Pay Periods: 26 per year
Bi-Weekly Gross x 26 / 12 = Monthly
Example: $1,200/bi-weekly x 26 / 12 = $2,600/month

Semi-Monthly Pay

  • + Hours Indicator: 86.67 or 88 hours
  • + Pay Periods: 24 per year
Semi-Monthly Gross x 24 / 12 = Monthly
Example: $1,200/semi-monthly x 24 / 12 = $2,400/month

Monthly Pay

  • + Paystub reflects: Paid once a month
  • + Check monthly income stated on paystub
Monthly Gross Amount = Monthly Income
Use the monthly gross amount directly from paystub

  • Step 1: Check paystub for hourly rate
  • Step 2: Check paystub for number of hours per week
  • Step 3: Apply the hourly income formula
Hourly Rate x Hours/Week x 52 Weeks / 12 Months = Monthly Income
Example: $7/hour x 32 hrs x 52 weeks / 12 months = $970.67/month

Calculation Tip

Always verify the number of hours worked per week matches the expected schedule. For full-time employees, this is typically 40 hours. Part-time hours will vary.

24-Month History Requirement

Bonus and Overtime income must have a 24-month history of receipt to be used for qualifying income.

  • Step 1: Compare previous 2 years W2s to current total YTD income
  • Step 2: Add YTD income + previous 2 years W2s
  • Step 3: Divide by total number of months used for income calculation
(YTD Income + Previous 2 Years W2s) / Total Months = Monthly Income
Example: $10,000 YTD (as of 3/31/21) + $75,000 in 24 month W2s / 27 months = $3,148.15/month

Month Count Explanation

In the example above, 27 months = 3 months YTD (Jan-Mar) + 24 months (2 full years of W2 history).

Commission Threshold

Commission income less than 25% of total income must have a 24-month receipt history. If commission is greater than 25% of income, refer the loan to underwriting for review.

  • Step 1: Compare previous year's W2 to current total YTD income
  • Step 2: Take average of YTD income + last year's W2
  • Step 3: Divide by total number of months used for income calculation
(YTD Income + Last Year W2) / Total Months = Monthly Income
Example: $5,000 YTD (as of 3/31/21) + $50,000 W2 / 15 months = $3,666.67/month

Leave & Earnings Statement (LES)

Military personnel are paid monthly via a Leave & Earnings Statement (LES). Always check paystub for regular and recurring income.

Eligible Income Types

  • + Base Income
  • + BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing)
  • + BAQ (Basic Allowance for Quarters)

Ineligible Income Types

  • x Hazard Pay
  • x Flight Pay
  • x Other non-recurring allowances
  • Step 1: Check LES for acceptable income types (Base, BAH, BAQ)
  • Step 2: Verify income is regular and recurring
  • Step 3: Calculate monthly income using YTD income from all acceptable sources

BAH & BAQ Gross-Up

*BAH & BAQ can be grossed up 25% when calculating qualifying income.

Seasonal & Union Workers

Calculate income for seasonal and union employees

Seasonal Workers

  • + Check application for job title
  • + Verify 2-year history on same job
  • + Examples: Landscapers, Construction workers
24-Month Average of W2 Income
Obtain 2 years W2s, take 24-month average. Unemployment income may be included.

Union Workers

  • + Check application for multiple jobs in past 2 years
  • + Review job description and title
  • + Obtain 2 years complete tax returns
(Income - Unreimbursed Expenses) / 24 = Monthly
Review Schedule A for unreimbursed business expenses. Subtract from each year's income, then average by 24 months.

Union Worker Documentation

Obtain 2 years of complete tax returns and all W2 forms. Review Schedule A for any unreimbursed business expenses. Subtract the unreimbursed business expenses from each year's income and average by 24 months.

Eligibility Restriction

Unemployment income may be used for seasonal and union workers only. Income must be received for at least 2 years with a likelihood to continue.

  • Step 1: Verify borrower is a seasonal or union worker
  • Step 2: Obtain 2 years of unemployment 1099 forms
  • Step 3: Calculate the 24-month unemployment income average
24-Month Average of Unemployment 1099 Income
Unemployment income must have a 2-year history of receipt to be used for qualifying.

Eligibility

If the borrower is of retirement age, Social Security Income can be used to qualify.

  • Step 1: Obtain one of the following documents:
    • Copy of Social Security award letter
    • Previous year's 1099 statement
    • Most recent bank statement to show receipt of income
  • Step 2: Verify if income is non-taxable
  • Step 3: If non-taxable, apply 25% gross-up

SSI Gross-Up

If income is verified as being non-taxable, the SSI can be grossed up by an additional 25%.

Documentation Type Purpose Status
SS Award Letter Confirms benefit amount and eligibility 1 Required
1099 Statement Previous year's income documentation Alternative
Bank Statement Shows receipt of income Alternative

Special Income Categories

Rental income, truck drivers, teachers, and alimony/child support

Rental Income

  • + Check application for all rental properties owned
  • + If tax returns submitted or 2-unit primary: Refer to Underwriting
  • + For other REO: Obtain 12-month rental leases
Lease Income x 75% = Qualifying Income
Use 75% of rental lease income amount to qualify

Truck Drivers

  • + Check paystub and application for job title
  • + Verify how borrower is paid (W2 vs 1099)
  • + If income on 1040 Schedule C: Consider Self-Employed
Obtain 2 years tax returns + W2s
If W2 with Schedule A expenses, calculate as Union Employee

Teachers

  • + Verify pay periods per year via paystub or VOE
  • + Must equal 12 months total
  • + For stipend/supplemental: Need 2-year history + proof of continuance
Average YTD Base Salary = Qualifying Income
Review current YTD paystub for pay periods and base salary

Alimony/Child Support

  • + Must be verified as regular and continuous
  • + Requires 12-month history of receipt
  • + Requires 3-year continuance
Document showing monthly income terms
Obtain: Divorce Decree, Separation Agreement, Court Decree, or other legal agreement

Truck Driver Classification

If truck driver's income is reported on 1040 Schedule C, the borrower should be considered as Self-Employed and processed accordingly.

Teacher Supplemental Income

If stipend and/or supplemental income is used for teachers, there must be a 2-year history of receipt and proof of continuance from employer to qualify.
