What is Salesforce CRM?

Salesforce is CMG Financial's customer relationship management platform that enables loan officers to:

  • Capture and manage mortgage leads from multiple sources
  • Track borrower interactions and communication history
  • Manage the sales pipeline from lead to funded loan
  • Automate follow-up tasks and reminders
  • Generate reports on conversion rates and sales metrics
  • Integrate seamlessly with CLEAR/Byte LOS systems

Key Salesforce Objects for Mortgage

Object Purpose Example
Lead Potential borrower not yet qualified Web inquiry, referral, purchased list
Contact Qualified individual with full profile Borrower, co-borrower, realtor partner
Account Organization or household Real estate office, builder, household
Opportunity Active loan in pipeline Purchase loan, refinance application
Task Action item or follow-up Call borrower, send pre-approval letter
Activity Logged interaction Completed call, email sent, meeting held

Pro Tip

Always log activities immediately after borrower interactions. This creates a complete communication history and helps other team members assist if needed.

Lead Status Progression

New -> Contacted -> Qualified -> Converted

Alternative paths: Unqualified (does not meet criteria) or Nurture (not ready now, future potential)

Lead Conversion Requirements

Before converting a lead, verify:

  • Borrower has confirmed interest in proceeding
  • Basic qualification criteria met (employment, credit estimate)
  • Loan purpose and property type identified
  • Timeline established (actively looking vs. 6+ months out)

Pipeline Hygiene

Review and update your pipeline weekly:

  • Update close dates that have slipped
  • Move stale opportunities to Nurture or Close Lost
  • Ensure all active loans have next steps scheduled
  • Verify loan amounts match current scenarios

Pro Tip

Use Salesforce's email integration to automatically log emails. Connect your Outlook or Gmail to Salesforce and select "Log to Salesforce" when sending borrower communications.

Task Best Practices

  • Set specific due dates, not "sometime this week"
  • Include context in task comments for future reference
  • Assign tasks to yourself immediately after creating
  • Review task queue at start of each day
  • Mark tasks complete promptly - overdue tasks hurt metrics

Loan Amount Base Loan Amount Bidirectional
Opportunity Stage Loan Status LOS -> SF
LOS Loan Number Loan Number LOS -> SF
Close Date Closing Date Bidirectional

Integration Features

  • One-Click Loan Creation: Push opportunity data to create loan in CLEAR/Byte
  • Status Sync: LOS milestone updates reflect in Salesforce automatically
  • Document Link: Quick access to loan documents from Salesforce
  • Rate Sheet Access: View current pricing without leaving SF
  • Condition Alerts: Outstanding conditions surface in Salesforce

Sync Considerations

  • Always verify borrower data accuracy before pushing to LOS
  • LOS is system of record once loan is created
  • Manual updates in LOS take priority over Salesforce
  • Sync runs every 15 minutes - not real-time
  • Contact IT if sync appears broken for more than 1 hour

Pro Tip

Use the "Open in LOS" button on any synced opportunity to jump directly to the loan in CLEAR or Byte. This saves time navigating between systems.
